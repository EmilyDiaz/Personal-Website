Overview

Problem

Current software does not allow for tracking of mail that is crucial for completing the lifecycle of cases. Mail must be managed through another, external software, which hinders the users' productivity as well as does not allow for in depth tracking of the mail's life cycle. Proper reporting needs were not being met for the mail team, which is crucial for auditing as well as performance reviews.

Challenges faced

  • MVP constraints were not established during the early solutioning phases with the client
  • Gathering of business requirements and designs progressed for nearly a year before ready to pass onto development, making communication stale in some areas
  • My first time leading a project made this a learn as you go situation. This was also not an enhancement to an existing process or page, this was an entirely new workflow.

Timeline

December 2022-Ongoing

Role

UX Designer

Solution breakdown

Intergrate mail tracking and management into the case manangement software, as well as allow for relationships to be established between mail and their corresponding cases.

Process

1. Mapping out the user flow

Our team at Booz Allen reached out to the mail team stakeholders for two rounds of demonstrations. Both demonstrations were conducted on the external software program the mail team used for intaking their mail and were run in addition to requirement gathering meetings.

We mapped out a rough user flow of how this external program worked and then worked out how this new flow would fit into the case management program. Our case managment program would recieve mail from the mail team's software on a defined schedule and place it in assignment queues.

Three major components were identified in the user flow:

1. Correspondence Review

The first component focuses on the communication between the two applications and being able to read the mail in the case management software.

Mail would be pulled from the external application and placed in a main queue in the case management application, where supervisors can assign out mail to specific users. These users would review the mail in this "raw" state before choosing to intake the mail. The review might involve requesting that a supervisor perform a specific action on the mail, such as merging it with another piece of mail, or editing certain details of the mail document.

2. Correspondence Intake

The second component focuses on the intake of this mail document.

If the mail document is a specific type of form, it can be intaken as a case. Any other type of mail can be intaken as a mail document, which is called the mail intake process. If needed, a user going through the mail intake process can add tasks to the mail, link related mail, and link related cases.

3.Correspondence Post-Intake

The third component focuses on the post-intake process of the mail document.

Once the intake process is done, a piece of mail becomes recorded in the case manangement system. If tasks were added during the intake process, then the mail is still in a active state until that task is closed out. The piece of mail can still be interacted with post-intake; new tasks can be added and relationships between the mail and other pieces of mail or cases can be edited.

Reflections

This was my first time in a UX lead role, and my first time working with the development team involved in this project. Having worked with a very different development team in the past, I had certain expectations that were not met with this new team and communication was a huge hurdle because of that. I quickly learned that, at this point in my career, I was not only not ready to lead a project of this scope and tumultuousness but that I also needed to work on my communication skills and to work on establishing a process to my design work.

Through the obstacles presented by this project, I have learned to ask more questions to developers and work with them to establish a process that works for both of us. My deliverables have shifted depending on what development team I'm working with to fit their needs as closely as possible. And I have slowly started to develop an understanding of how to speak in developer terms. This project was half way through development when it was put on hold but I have learned from the experience immensely and have been able to apply what I've learned to other projects that I have worked on since then.
