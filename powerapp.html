X&D Advisor Hub Powerapp

Problem

The Advisor Hub has been active since 2023 but there has been a lack of consistent engagement from the users. Upon joining the X&D advisor hub as a lead, I suggested introducing a way of tracking advisorship progress for both user types and gamifying this progress in order to improve both aquistion and retention. This whole effort was a volunteer and leadership experience through the X&D Advisor Hub at Booz Allen; this was not part of my job and had to be after hours.

Challenges faced

  • Limitations of Powerapps and Powerautomate licenses prevented the pull of information from Microsoft Bookings.
  • Microsoft Bookings' email format did not provide the information expected in order to automate pull of information for advisees and advisors.

Timeline

October 2025-Ongoing

Released to production. Undergoing pilot testing by users.

Solution

Utlize the Microsoft ecosystem to create a gamified dashboard that is not only easily accessible for users, but is secure on our company network and is able to be developed fast and easily by someone with a non-programmer background.

1. Understanding existing processes

The main process for a user to participate in the X&D Advisor Hub program was, as follows:

  1. Attend an onboarding session hosted at a quarterly cadence by the Advisor Hub
  2. Recieve an onboarding survey from the Hub and input information such as, but not limited to:
    • Level of UX/UI experience
    • Whether the user would like to be an advisor, advisee, or both.
    • What topics the user would like to advise or be advised on.
  3. Once the survey was submitted, the user would then recieve a link to the X&D Bookings Page, a site where onboarded users can set their schedules as advisors and schedule meetings as advisees with an advisor of their choosing.

The users who were onboarded as advisees or both could then go to the Bookings Page and select a topic they wished to have advising in. Once a topic was selected, they could select a preferred meeting date and time. A dropdown would then conditionally populate advisors based on selections the user made in the previous two sections.

Once the user submits their scheduled meeting, they get a calender invite through their Microsoft Outlook.

2. Enhancing the process

Each advisor would have their own Bookings account, with a central Bookings calendar shared amongst the advisors. Therefore, they could see upcoming meetings with an advisee, but there was no easy way to track which advisees they had meetings with. Furthermore, the definition of what is considered an advisee or advisor could be subjective based on the user:

Is it enough to consider a person an advisee or advisor with just one meeting? Or would there have to be more of an established cadence?

There was also a lack of continous engagement, where there'd be a meeting set up only once between different sets of advisee and advisor, as well as a lack of onboarding interest.

I began to research the definition of gamification, what elements or standards it consisted of, and examples of gamification in the workplace. Once I had a proper understanding of that, I looked for options that were compliant with the security standards at Booz Allen:

1. Workday

Workday is currently used internally by Booz Allen staff. It has its own version of a mentorship program, where advisees can send advisorships requests to potential advisors.

There was also a badge system in place, but these badges were tied to learning courses and not related to the native mentorship functionality.

However, Workday did not offer alot of customization options or potential for growth. Navigation to different areas of Workday, including these mentorship and badges functionality, seemed rather daunting and was too large in scale for what the program was looking for in a dashboard.

2. Figma Make

Figma Make is Figma's attempt at using AI and is currently still in Beta. Pairing the versatility of Make with integrated connections such as Supabase offered an enticing possibility.

Furthermore, Make would offer a friendlier environment to the other leads given their non-programmer backgrounds. The only caveat to this would be the learning curve to learn SQL for using Supabase.

However, I could not find any documentation from my company attesting to whether or not Supabase was safe to use. The other issue would be how long it would take, realistically, to get to MVP state of the app given the learning curve for SQL.

3. Microsoft Ecosystem

Microsoft 365 and all of its products are offered to our company and personnel is added to our company through the use of Entra ID. Given this, the connectivity of all products and personnel information access made this option a seamless one. The use of Powerapps, Sharepoint List, Bookings, and Powerautomate could all be used to create the dashboard we'd want but there were some caveats:

The original idea of connecting Microsoft Bookings to either Powerapps or Powerautomate in order to access meeting information was quickly squashed given the type of license Booz Allen had for all three of these products. Microsoft Outlook had to be relied on instead.

There would also be a similar learning curve for learning how to use PowerFX, the psuedo coding language for Powerapps, in order to expand the MVP. Powerautomate would also have its own learning curve, though expectations of change after initial flow setup were low.

Inspiration for the app camme mainly from video games and persona cards, the latter being an artifact used by UX designers on the early stages of design. Persona cards are used to identify key user types and humanize these user types, often giving them a name, personality, and likes and dislikes.

A flow was drafted in mural to represent the MVP state of the app. MVP functionalties and UI screens were proposed to the other leads with the use of mockups and this flow. Once shown, the MVP state was agreed upon by the leads, with some proposed, initial changes. MVP functionalities included, but not limited to:

  • List of advisees and advisors
  • A way to send feedback to the X&D Hub about the program and the app
  • Goals

VS

Early mockups

UI wise, we weighed what we wanted the dashboard to focus on: The logged in user's profile or the logged in user's advisees and advisors information.

Both pieces of information were crucial, but we ultimately decided to put the logged in user's profile front and center, with navigation to advisee and advisor pages.
